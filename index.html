Research Experience

Climate Computing Research

During the fall 2024 semester, I worked with a group of three other researchers to determine what impacts extreme Arctic Oscillation would have on the northern Atlantic Ocean. Over the course of the semester, we developed our research question, tested numerous climate models, and presented our findings at the annual FIRE Summit at the University of Maryland. Our research was conducted as a part of the First-Year Innovation and Research Experience at the University of Maryland, specifically within the Climate Computing stream of the program fascilitated by Dr. Sara Strey.

Research Mentoring

I am currently a Peer Research Mentor with the Climate Computing Stream of FIRE. I serve as a mentor to freshmen Climate Computing Researchers who are beginning their own research projects and need help with the fundamentals of research and intricacies of running climate models.
